welcome to Cell division and cell cycle
First stage of mitosis in which chromosomes condense, become visible, and spindle fibers begin to form.
The nuclear envelope breaks down, and spindle fibers attach to chromosomes via kinetochores, moving them toward the cell’s equator.
Chromosomes align at the cell’s equator, attached to spindle fibers from opposite poles via their kinetochores.
Sister chromatids separate at the centromere and are pulled toward opposite poles of the cell by the spindle fibers.
Chromosomes reach opposite poles, decondense into chromatin, and nuclear envelopes reform around each set.
The cytoplasm divides, producing two separate daughter cells, each with its own nucleus and identical set of chromosomes.
